## Tone Profile
- Vocabulary: Accessible and conversational, uses financial jargon but always explains it inline ("the Full Retirement Sum, also known as the FRS"). Avoids overly academic language. Uses concrete dollar amounts and percentages to ground abstract concepts ($4,000/month, 3% increment, 15% EPS growth).
- Style: Explanatory and data-driven. Opens with a hook (news event, provocative claim, or Reddit post), then systematically breaks the topic into numbered sections. Heavy use of "what if" scenarios and worked examples. Frequently compares options side-by-side (e.g., FRS vs ERS, 1980 vs 2026). Likes to present both bull and bear cases before giving his own position.
- Formality: Semi-casual. Professional enough for financial content but peppered with colloquialisms ("hoo ha", "sucky", "all the customers run out of the store screaming"). Speaks directly to the viewer with "you" and "I" framing. Feels like a knowledgeable friend explaining over coffee, not a lecturer.
- Humor: Light and situational rather than comedic. Uses hyperbole for effect ("forced to work until death", "including the poor penguins"). Occasional self-aware asides. Never sarcastic or mean-spirited.
- Catchphrases and recurring patterns:
  - "Anyway, that's all for this video. Hope you found this useful. Like, share, and subscribe as I'll be posting new videos every week."
  - "But first..." (transition to context/backstory before the main point)
  - "So the question isn't [simple framing] but [nuanced framing]" -- reframing technique
  - "In short..." or "The bottom line is this..." (summary before closing)
  - "But what do you think? [question]. Drop a comment below."
  - Numbered scenario analysis ("Scenario 1... Scenario 2... Scenario 3...")
  - Sponsor integrations mid-video with personal referral codes (KELVIN88, kvli.link links)
