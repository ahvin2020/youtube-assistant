# Thumbnail Style Reference

Based on visual analysis of 110 thumbnails: 30 from Kelvin Learns Investing (own channel)
and 80 from 18 competitor/reference channels, ranked by outlier score.

**Date**: 2026-03-07
**Own channel sample**: Top 30 videos by views (outlier range 1.14x-3.0x)
**Competitor sample**: Top 80 videos by outlier score across 18 channels (outlier range 1.91x-9.33x)

---

## Current Brand Identity (Own Channel)

### The Template
```
[Yellow border with rounded-corner dark inner panel]
    [LEFT 60%: 2-4 word bold yellow ALL CAPS text]
    [LEFT 60%: Red banner with white secondary text]
    [RIGHT 40%: Kelvin cutout, gray t-shirt, glasses]
    [RIGHT 40%: Confident expression, open palm gesture]
    [BACKGROUND: Dark with optional financial chart overlay]
```

### Signature Elements (present in 80%+ of thumbnails)
1. ~~**Yellow border + rounded-corner dark panel**~~ — legacy element, no longer required
2. **Subject right, text left** — near-universal layout (28/30)
3. **Yellow bold ALL CAPS text** — primary text, condensed sans-serif (Impact/Bebas style)
4. **Red banner with white text** — secondary message stamp (~20/30)
5. **Dark background** — high contrast with yellow text (~20/30)
6. **Gray crew-neck t-shirt + rectangular glasses** — consistent wardrobe
7. **Cutout compositing** — green-screen removal, composited onto designed backgrounds

### Text System (Two-Tier)
- **Primary**: Yellow, massive (40-50% of frame width for a single word), upper-left
- **Secondary**: White on red rectangular banner, directly below primary
- **Special formats**: Dollar amounts ($2,000, $53,325), percentages (5%), years (2026)
- **Word count**: 2-4 words, never a full sentence
- Text never overlaps face; may overlap hand/body

### Color Palette
| Color | Usage | Hex Range |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Yellow | Borders, primary text, backgrounds | #FFD700-#FFFF00 |
| Black | Inner panel backgrounds | #000000-#1A1A1A |
| Red | Text banners, negative indicators | #CC0000-#FF0000 |
| White | Secondary text (inside red banners) | #FFFFFF |
| Green | Positive indicators, chart lines, money | #00CC00-#33FF33 |

### Expression Library
| Expression | Usage | Frequency | Performance |
|------------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Confident/knowing smirk + open palm | Default, "let me show you" | ~15/30 | **Highest** (all top 3 use this) |
| Concerned/worried + hand on head | Question/problem content | ~6/30 | Mid-range (1.29x-1.57x) |
| Shocked/alarmed + hand over mouth | Crash/warning content | ~4/30 | Mid-to-low (1.24x-1.32x) |
| Open-mouthed explaining | Educational/how-to | ~5/30 | Mixed |

### What Drives Performance (Own Channel)
1. **Specific dollar amounts/percentages** — all top 6 thumbnails feature concrete numbers
2. **Confident expression** — outperforms alarmed/shocked at every tier
3. **Template consistency** — no layout experiments in top performers
4. **Red banner with action words** — "PASSIVE INCOME", "GUARANTEED" > questions
5. **Singapore-specific + numbers** — CPF content with dollar figures = strongest combo
6. **Dark backgrounds + financial charts** — safe, consistent default

### What Underperforms (Own Channel)
- Alarmed/shocked expressions (hand over mouth) cluster 1.24x-1.32x
- Vague text ("GET READY", "IT'S STARTING") vs specific numbers
- Layout deviations (collage, interview format)
- Question-only text without a number hook

## Competitor Patterns (80 Thumbnails, 18 Channels)

### Universal Formula (Finance/Education YouTube)
The single most reliable formula across all channels and outlier tiers:

1. **One large face** — 30-60% of frame height, well-lit against dark background
2. **Asymmetric composition** — face on one side, text/graphics on the other
3. **Minimal text** — 0-4 words, bold condensed sans-serif, ALL CAPS
4. **High contrast** — universal across every top performer, no exceptions
5. **One supporting visual element** — prop, icon, chart, or news overlay (never more than one)
6. **Color-coded emotion** — red=danger, green=money, yellow=wealth, blue=trust

### Composition Rules
- **Right-side face placement** is dominant across all batches (~60% of thumbnails)
- **Left-side face** is the second pattern, used by some channels consistently
- **Cutout compositing** is standard practice (subject removed from original BG, placed on designed one)
- **Single-person compositions** outperform multi-person at the top tier

### The Simplicity Signal
The clearest differentiator between 3x+ and 2.4x-2.6x outlier tiers is **visual simplicity**.
Top performers communicate one idea with maximum clarity. Lower performers try to communicate
more and end up with busier compositions. This holds across all 80 competitor thumbnails.

| Element | Top Tier (3x+) | Lower Tier (2x-2.5x) |
|---------|----------------|----------------------|
| Text | 0-3 words | 4-8 words |
| Background | Clean/simple | Busy/layered |
| People | 1 person | 2-3 people |
| Visual elements | 1 clear hook | Multiple competing elements |
| Expression | Single strong emotion | Mixed/moderate |

### Text Patterns That Work
- **Zero text** can work when the image itself creates the curiosity gap (6x+ outlier tier)
- **Dollar amounts in green** — most effective finance-specific text hook
- **Short provocative questions** (2-4 words) — "What Went Wrong?", "Is this legal?"
- **Embedded text** (in props like tweet cards, news banners) outperforms floating overlay text
- **One accent color word** — key word in yellow/red/green, rest in white

### Expression Patterns
- **Concerned/skeptical** outperforms happy/positive at the top tier (negativity bias)
- **Avg outlier with negative expression**: ~2.01x vs ~1.95x with positive
- **Direct-to-camera gaze** in ~70% of thumbnails
- Common gestures: prayer hands (Andrei Jikh signature), pointing, hand on face, props in hand

### Background Treatment
- **Dark backgrounds** in ~70% of top performers
- **Designed/composited** > real-world scenes for performance (2.00x vs 1.95x avg)
- **Stock charts as texture** — semi-transparent overlays for atmosphere, not readable data
- **Simple backgrounds** cluster at higher outlier scores
- **Radial vignette** (dark edges, lighter center) used in ~30% for natural focus

### The Outlier Ceiling: Scene-Based vs Face-Based
An important finding from the extreme outlier tier (6x+):
- The top 4 extreme outliers are ALL **scene/object-based** with no prominent face
- The face-forward formula reliably delivers 3x-5x but rarely breaks 6x
- Scene-based thumbnails work when the subject is visually recognizable and inherently intriguing
  (a cracked casino building, a 102-year-old doing yoga, an immigration raid)
- For finance content, face-based is the safer bet; scene-based is higher ceiling but riskier

## Channel Signature Breakdown

### Finance Channels (Most Relevant)

**Humphrey Yang** — Clean, bright, product-photography aesthetic
- White/gray backgrounds, cutout on right, bold sans-serif text
- Illustrated/AI graphics paired with real face
- Green for money amounts, confident smirk + pointing gesture
- Tier list format for ranking content

**Damien Talks Money** — Cinematic, moody, minimal text
- Dark navy backgrounds, face on left, concerned expression
- Physical props as data visualization (coin stacks as bar charts, brass scale)
- Maximum 3 words text. Lets composition tell the story
- Film-quality color grading distinguishes from peers

**Graham Stephan** — News media framing, reactive content
- Social media screenshots as content elements (tweets, news banners)
- Fire/dramatic backgrounds, distressed expression
- Split compositions (political figure vs creator)

**Andrei Jikh** — Templated, no-text, center-face
- Identical prayer-hands pose across all videos
- Face dead center, political/financial figures flanking both sides
- Zero text on thumbnail. Dark, gold-heavy palette
- Most templated brand in the sample

**Mark Tilbury** — Teaching/whiteboard, dollar amounts
- Blue shirt consistent, face on left, dark backgrounds
- Whiteboard/chalkboard aesthetic, stick figure illustrations
- Dollar amounts prominent, social proof via tweet cards
- Green = money, chalk-style drawings

**Ben Felix** — Academic/research aesthetic
- Layered screenshots of papers, comments, calculators as background
- Enormous outlined/3D text dominating the frame
- Face relatively small (bottom-right). Skeptical expression
- No other channel uses this approach — signals intellectual credibility

**Minority Mindset** — Maximum urgency, news-broadcast style
- Giant face (chin to turban fills frame), dark backgrounds
- Bold yellow text on black bottom bar (signature: "PREPARE", "PREPARE NOW")
- Red and green color coding for market direction
- Breaking news graphics and political figures

**The Money Guy Show** — Multi-host panel format
- Two hosts flanking colorful infographic-style data visualization
- Blue background consistent, 3D emoji-style icons
- Two-color text (white base + one red/green keyword)
- Most visually "busy" but branded consistently

**Ziet Invests** — No face, investigation aesthetic
- Never shows creator's face. Object/scene/brand-centric
- Moody, dark, atmospheric (night scenes, cork boards)
- Short provocative question text ("What Went Wrong?")
- Red string investigation board for scandal/analysis content

**The Fifth Person** — Singapore-specific, text-dominant
- Two hosts flanking center text
- Largest text-to-image ratio in the set
- Dark red/green backgrounds, gold text accents
- Prohibition icons for "don't" framing

### Non-Finance Channels (Style Inspiration)

**MrBeast** — Zero text, maximum spectacle
- Never uses text. Full-body action/scene composition
- Extreme production quality. Environmental storytelling
- Expressions match content tone (big smile vs distressed)

**Veritasium** — Concept-first, face optional
- Thumbnail IS the visual hook/mystery (macro photo, illustration)
- Willing to de-emphasize host face entirely for conceptual intrigue
- Serif fonts, documentary aesthetic. "$400,000,000" as hook

**Ali Abdaal** — Lifestyle/real environment
- Real room backgrounds, bright airy lighting
- Serif/editorial typography, mixed case (not always ALL CAPS)
- Props (phone, whiteboard) integrated naturally
- "Thoughtful creator" positioning vs "shocking news" positioning

**Johnny Harris** — Journalistic, real-world footage
- Documentary footage frames. NYT branding watermark
- Orange highlight boxes as annotation elements
- No constructed graphics — authenticity-first approach

## Actionable Recommendations

### Keep (Working Well)
- Right-side cutout, left-side text layout
- Yellow/red two-tier text system
- Confident expression as default
- Specific dollar amounts/percentages as text hooks

### Evolve (Competitor-Informed)
1. **Reduce text further** — competitors' top performers use 0-3 words; own channel averages 3-5. Try cutting to 2 words + red banner
2. **Experiment with concerned expression** — competitor data shows worried/skeptical outperforms happy for finance content. Currently underused in own thumbnails (confident is default). Test worried expression on crash/warning content
3. **Try scene-based for the right topics** — no-face, object-centric thumbnails have the highest outlier ceiling (6x+). Good candidates: Singapore landmarks, recognizable brands, investigation-board layouts for scandal/analysis content
4. **Embed text in props** — tweet cards, news banners, app screenshots feel more organic than floating text overlays. Competitors use this increasingly
5. **Physical objects as data viz** — coin stacks, cash piles, scales as visual metaphors (Damien Talks Money approach). More tangible than chart overlays
6. **One accent color per thumbnail** — competitors emphasize exactly one keyword in a contrasting color (yellow, green, or red). Currently the own channel uses yellow + red consistently, which is good, but could be more intentional about which word gets the accent

### Avoid
- More than 4 words of overlay text
- Multiple competing visual elements (multi-person, multi-graphic)
- Light/white backgrounds for serious/crash content (dark outperforms)
- Generic stock imagery or abstract AI backgrounds (zero top performers use this)
- Neutral/mild expressions (clear emotional signal always outperforms neutral)
- Cluttered layouts without clear visual hierarchy

### Experimental Ideas (Higher Risk, Higher Ceiling)
- **Investigation board aesthetic** for analysis/scandal content (red strings, pinned documents)
- **Fake news chyron/tweet embed** for reactive/breaking content (Graham Stephan style)
- **Macro object close-up** with no face for the right topic (Veritasium style)
- **Physical prop in hand** — holding relevant object (cash, phone app, document) adds authenticity
- **Chalk/whiteboard aesthetic** for educational deep-dives (Mark Tilbury style)
